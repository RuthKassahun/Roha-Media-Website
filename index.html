Roha Media and Communication Works

Developing a pluralistic, strong, professional media sector is fundamental to realizing Ethiopia’s democratic transition. The media must be able to report and disseminate accurate information to Ethiopia’s citizenry, hold the government to account for any injustices or abuses of power, and play a positive role during democratization by creating awareness about how citizens can engage to encourage reform.

Decades of dictatorship have made this challenge a great one. Though Ethiopia’s media sector is gaining strength and the media reform process is ongoing, there is still a long way to go. Though new media houses are emerging, they lack the economic and human capacity to produce ample independent content. The government is becoming increasingly open with information, but the transition from a highly repressive state to a transparent state will not happen overnight. Moreover, it is critical to assess not only the extent to which media space has opened, but also what journalists are doing with that space. It is after this concept our organization established.

About Roha Media And Communication Works

Roha Media & Communication Works (RMCW) is non-government, non-political; an independent media organization founded by Asmamaw Gizaw (a former BKCIS at Harvard University & Reagan-Fascell Democracy Fellow) a well known journalist who was imprisoned for defending freedom of expression by the previous Ethiopian administration. Our organization applied to the concerned government organization to have the legal license to operate according in to the law of the land. Accordingly, our organization as an independent media institute also registered and accredited by Ethiopian Broadcast Authority with certificate no. 96132.

Because of decades of systematic attacks on freedom of the press by the previous administration officials the status of the independent media in Ethiopia is very much threatened. Accordingly, our organization aspires to play its role to change this difficult situation. In order to achieve our objectives we work with local & international partners in promoting democracy and freedom of expression by empowering journalists and independent media for a better Media pluralism environment.

RMCW as an independent media organization in Ethiopia it aspires to holds powerful state and non-state interests accountable, serving as a watchdog for the public interest. The vision of our organization is glimpsing a population that has alternative information source and nurtured by information. Based in Addis Ababa we tried to reach most part of Ethiopia based on the project we implement.

Focus Areas

Based on the detail assessments we undertake with several stakeholders here in Ethiopia, our priority is now focused in the strengthening of independent media sector in Ethiopia through several interventions listed briefly below:

Training and capacity building

we collaborate with non government organization or other international donors to facilitate training that empower journalists and other media practitioners.

Policy advocacy

In order to see a vibrant private media we engage in advocacy for overall legal and institutionalize support for private media & beyond.

Addis media lab

we are trying to set up the first in its kind a media lab that could help the media in Ethiopia in a very innovative way.

Networking

by facilitating a platform where editors, journalist, researchers, citizen journalists, media experts and other media practitioners come together to work together for a better media environment.

Learning resources

To fill the gap caused by the two decades of systematic attack on the private media we are working hard to organize and distribute learning resources both online & offline.

Content production

we produce a high quality media program so that to ensure media plays a positive role in Ethiopia’s democratization & reconciliation process by presenting an exemplify in depth report on conflict and transition sensitive journalism.

Media as a Tool for Democratization and Peace building

we strives to support Ethiopia’s democratization process by encouraging that traditional & new media sources provide accurate, impartial, and conflict-sensitive information to citizens and that citizens view media as a channel for their participation in public life.
